Overview

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro, born on 5 February 1985 in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, is one of the greatest footballers in history. Known worldwide as CR7, he has redefined excellence, longevity, and professionalism in the game. Ronaldo began his career as a teenage prodigy in Portugal and rose to global fame with Manchester United, where he won his first Ballon d’Or in 2008. His move to Real Madrid in 2009 made him a global superstar — he became the club’s all-time top scorer, won four UEFA Champions League titles, and numerous domestic trophies. Later, he added success in Italy with Juventus, made a dramatic return to Manchester United, and currently plays for Al-Nassr in Saudi Arabia. Beyond his club career, Ronaldo has been the heart and captain of Portugal’s national team, leading them to their first major international trophies — Euro 2016 and the UEFA Nations League 2019. With over 900 career goals (1000 soon), five Ballon d’Ors, and countless records, Ronaldo’s influence goes far beyond the pitch. He is a symbol of hard work, self-discipline, fitness, and an unrelenting will to win.
